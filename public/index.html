Why Pro Con

01

Pro Con is family-operated across two generations. You aren't handed to a rotating crew of subs you never met — the owner who walked your project is the one making the calls on site, every day it's being built.

02

Built warmer than code requires.

Most builders insulate to the code minimum and stop. We don't. Pro Con's walls and attics are built past the minimum on purpose — because a house in Duluth earns its keep in January, and the heating bill is yours, not ours.

03

A deck that doesn't grey out in three seasons.

We build decks in Arbor Wood Co. thermally modified wood — real wood, USA-sourced, put through a three-phase thermal process that pulls out the moisture and sugars that make ordinary lumber cup, swell, and rot. Most go up in one to three weeks.

Before you call

Questions, answered.

How long does it take to build a custom home in Duluth?

Most single-family custom homes take eight to twelve months from footings to final walkthrough; duplex builds usually run ten to fourteen months. Pro Con insulates above Minnesota code minimums, which matters for a Northern Minnesota winter.

What areas do you build in?

We build across Northern Minnesota — Duluth, Hermantown, Cloquet, Two Harbors, Proctor, Esko, Carlton, Hibbing, Grand Rapids, Virginia and surrounding communities. We're based in Duluth, MN 55804.

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes. Pro Con LLC is a licensed Minnesota contractor (license #QB807406), is insured, and backs its work with a workmanship guarantee.

How long does it take to build a deck?

Most decks take one to three weeks. We build in Arbor Wood Co. thermally modified wood — a USA-sourced decking made with a three-phase thermal modification process that resists moisture and movement.

Do you build detached garages?

Yes. Most detached garages take four to eight weeks. A standard two-car garage is typically twenty-four feet wide by twenty-four feet deep, and we also build larger garages and outbuildings.

Can you add on to my existing home?

Yes. We build additions worked into your existing home — extra bedrooms, bathrooms, even a sauna. One recent Duluth project added a bedroom and a bathroom/sauna into an existing space.
