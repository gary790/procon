The room

The damage
you can't see.

A leak, dated tile, or a layout that never worked is the reason most people call. But the real problem with bathrooms is the one you don't notice: the remodel that looks finished and fails behind the wall, quietly soaking the framing until the floor tells on it. A bathroom is mostly waterproofing wearing tile.

Pro Con builds the part you can't see first. Dan Bruckelmyer quotes the room, settles the layout, and is on the job through demo, waterproofing, and finish — and yes, Pro Con builds bathrooms around saunas, too. One recent project worked a bathroom and sauna into an existing space. Here's what a Pro Con bathroom includes:

  • Demo & disposal. The old bathroom out and hauled away, down to sound framing before anything new goes back.
  • Waterproofing done right. A waterproof shower and wet-wall base built before tile — the step that decides whether the room lasts.
  • Tile work. Floors, walls, and shower set straight and sealed, not just stuck up to look good on day one.
  • Plumbing fixtures. Shower valves, tub, toilet, and faucets set to the new layout and tested before the walls close.
  • Vanity & finishes. Vanity, counter, lighting, and trim — the surfaces you touch every morning.
  • Ventilation, and an optional sauna. Proper exhaust so moisture leaves the room — and a built-in sauna space when that's the plan.

Bathroom questions

Answered.

Why do so many remodeled bathrooms fail behind the wall?

Because the waterproofing under the tile was skipped or rushed. A bathroom can look finished and still be wicking water into the framing. We build a waterproof shower and wet-wall base before any tile is set, so the part you can't see is the part we get right first.

Can you build a sauna into our bathroom?

Yes — we build bathrooms around saunas. On one recent Duluth-area project we worked a bathroom and sauna into an existing space. If you want a sauna as part of the remodel, we plan the framing, ventilation, and finishes for it from the start rather than tacking it on at the end.

Will you fix a bad layout, or just replace what's there?

We'll rework the layout. Moving the vanity, resizing the shower, or relocating plumbing is part of the design step before demo. We settle the new layout with you up front so the plumbing and tile are set to the plan, not to wherever the old fixtures happened to sit.

What does a bathroom remodel cost in Duluth?

It's quoted to the scope — a tub-to-shower conversion in Hermantown and a full gut with a sauna in Two Harbors are not the same job. We give you a free written estimate after seeing the room, and the schedule is set before demo so you know what you're signing up for.

Is the owner actually on the job?

Yes. Dan is on site for the work, not relaying it through a crew you never met. Pro Con is licensed in Minnesota under #QB807406 and insured, and every remodel is backed by a workmanship guarantee.
