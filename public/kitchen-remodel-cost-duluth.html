What a kitchen remodel costs in Duluth.

The honest numbers

Three tiers, plainly priced.

Planning ranges for the Duluth area in 2026 — your kitchen’s size, layout, and finishes move them. The written estimate is free.

~$30k–$45k

The refresh

Cabinets stay, everything around them improves: counters, backsplash, sink and faucet, lighting, paint, hardware, maybe flooring. Biggest visual change per dollar.

~$45k–$70k

The full remodel

New cabinets, counters, flooring, lighting, and appliances in the existing layout. The plumbing and wiring stay put — which is what keeps this tier out of the top one.

~$70k–$90k+

Down to the studs

Walls move, the sink relocates, an island appears. Layout changes mean plumbing, electrical, and sometimes structure — the kitchen your house was never built with.

Where the money goes.  Cabinets usually take 25–35% of the budget; counters and appliances come next. Moving plumbing or walls is what pushes a project up a tier — we’ll tell you honestly whether your layout is worth changing.

The ProCon way

How it stays
on budget.

Kitchen budgets don’t blow up on cabinets — they blow up on surprises and drift. Three habits prevent both: a written, itemized estimate with allowances spelled out before demo day; a scope locked before the first cabinet is ordered; and change orders priced in writing before the work happens.

One more that matters in an older Duluth home: an honest look behind the walls early. Knob-and-tube wiring, undersized plumbing, or a soft subfloor are far cheaper to plan for than to discover. Dan walks every kitchen personally before the estimate — nearly three decades in, very little behind a wall surprises him.

Most full remodels run 4–8 weeks of construction, same crew start to finish, with a temporary kitchen set up so your house keeps working. The work is backed by a workmanship guarantee, licensed MN #QB807406.

Kitchen cost questions

Answered.

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Duluth, MN?

Most Duluth-area kitchen remodels in 2026 land roughly between $30,000 and $90,000: a refresh runs about $30k–$45k, a full remodel $45k–$70k, and a down-to-the-studs rework with layout changes $70k–$90k or more. The written estimate for your kitchen is free.

What’s the most expensive part?

Cabinets, usually — commonly 25–35% of the budget — followed by counters and appliances. Moving the sink, range, or a wall is what pushes a project up a tier, because plumbing and electrical follow the layout.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

Most full remodels take 4 to 8 weeks of construction once materials are on hand. Cabinet lead times are the long pole — custom cabinetry can take 6–12 weeks to arrive — so first call to finished kitchen is usually 3–5 months.

Can I live in the house during the remodel?

Yes — almost everyone does. We help set up a temporary kitchen, contain the dust, and sequence the work to keep the disruption window short. The same ProCon crew is on the job start to finish.
