How it works

No surprises.
That's the process.

Handing someone your home for months takes trust. Here's exactly what happens between your first call and the day we hand it back — six steps, each one in writing, with the owner on the job the whole way.

Start with Step 1 (218) 348-2076

The six steps

From first call
to guarantee.

01

The first call

You call or send the form. Dan picks up or calls back personally — no call center, no sales rep. You talk through what you want to build, roughly where, and what matters most to you. Five minutes is usually enough to know whether it's a fit.

Timeline: 1–2 days

02

The walkthrough

Dan comes to the property. He looks at what you're starting with — grade, drainage, structure, sun, snow load, access — because in Northern Minnesota the lot writes half the plan. You'll get straight talk about what's possible and what it'll fight you on.

Timeline: Within the first week

03

The written estimate

A real number, in writing, free. Scope spelled out line by line — what's included, what's allowance, what's excluded — so there are no soft numbers waiting to surprise you in month four.

Timeline: About a week after walkthrough

04

Plans & permits

Once you say go, we handle drawings, engineering where it's needed, and the permit file with your city or county. You sign off on the plan before anything is ordered.

Timeline: 2–8 weeks depending on scope

05

The build

One crew, owner on the job. We sequence the work, manage inspections, and keep you updated in plain language — photos and a call, not a portal you have to chase. Weather is planned for, not used as an excuse.

Timeline: Decks in days; kitchens in weeks; homes in months — your estimate carries the real dates

06

Walkthrough & guarantee

We walk every inch together before we call it done. Punch list gets cleared, then the workmanship guarantee backs the job after we've left. If something's not right, you call Dan — the same person who built it.

Timeline: At completion — and after

The other half of the promise

What you'll never get
from ProCon.

A soft number

Estimates are written, itemized, and honest about allowances. The price you plan around is the price we work to.

A rotating cast

One crew start to finish. The person who shook your hand at the walkthrough is the person on your roof in week six.

Radio silence

You hear from us before you have to ask. Photos, plain language, and a call when a decision needs you.

A disappearing act

The workmanship guarantee means the job isn't over when we load the trailer. Something's not right? Call Dan.

Step 1 takes five minutes.
