Building in Hermantown

Up the hill
from the lake.

Hermantown sits northwest of Duluth and uphill from the lakeshore, and that extra elevation changes the job. The town pulls down notably more snow than downtown Duluth does, so roof load and drifting stop being a footnote and become a number you design around — pitch, framing, and bracing all sized for the weight that piles up at this height.

The flip side of being newer and more suburban is space. Lots here run larger than the tight parcels down in the city, which leaves real room to build — the oversized garage, the shop, the detached outbuilding that simply won't fit on a Duluth hillside lot. And because parts of Hermantown run on well and septic instead of city utilities, we work the well, the field, and the setbacks into the site plan from day one instead of discovering them later.

On the map

Right next door, just up the hill.

One thing the
elevation costs you.

The same height that gives Hermantown bigger lots and a view also dumps more snow on your roof than the lakeshore ever sees, and the wind up here drifts it into deep, uneven loads on one side of the ridge. A roof framed to a generic spec carries that off-center weight all winter, season after season, and you don't find out it was undersized until something sags.

We design for the real load up front — steeper pitch where it sheds, framing and bracing sized for the drift, and detailing at the eaves and valleys built to take it. On a new build, an addition, or a big garage, that's part of the structure from the start, not a repair you pay for later.

Hermantown questions

Answered.

Why does Hermantown get more snow than Duluth, and how do you build for it?

Hermantown sits uphill and northwest of Duluth at a higher elevation than the lakeshore, and that extra height pulls down notably more snow than downtown sees. We design roofs around the real snow load and drifting — pitch, framing, and bracing sized for the weight that actually lands up here, not a generic lakeside number.

Can you take advantage of a larger Hermantown lot for a big garage or outbuilding?

That's one of the best parts of building in Hermantown. The development is newer and more suburban than Duluth, with larger lots and more room to work — so the oversized garage, the shop, or the detached outbuilding you couldn't fit on a tight city parcel is on the table here. We plan the site so the main house and the outbuilding sit right together.

Do you build on well and septic in Hermantown?

Yes. Parts of Hermantown are on well and septic rather than city utilities, so we factor the well location, septic field, and setbacks into the site plan from the start. Getting the system placed and sized right up front keeps it from boxing in your driveway, garage, or future additions later.

How quickly can you get to a Hermantown project from Duluth?

Fast — Hermantown is right next door to our Duluth base, just up the hill, so we're effectively local to every project here. Estimates are free and in writing, with a response within 24 hours.
