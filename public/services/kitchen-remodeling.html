The room you live in

The kitchen is where
the family ends up.

The cooking is only half of it. The kitchen is where homework gets done, where everyone crowds in during a party, where the morning starts. When it's dated or too tight to move in, the whole house feels it — and the thing that stops most people from fixing it is the fear of a remodel that drags on with nobody clearly in charge.

ProCon runs the kitchen with one crew and the owner on site, so there's one person accountable from demo to the final fixture. Dan Bruckelmyer quotes it, plans the layout with you, and is there while it's built. Here's what a ProCon kitchen covers:

  • Demo & disposal. The old kitchen out and the debris hauled away — with the rest of the house protected from dust and traffic.
  • Layout changes. Walls moved, islands added, the work triangle reworked so the room fits how you cook — not just new doors on the old plan.
  • Cabinetry. Cabinets measured, set level, and fit to the space, including around the corners every real kitchen has.
  • Countertops. Counters templated and installed to your selection — the surface you'll use every single day.
  • Tile & backsplash. Backsplash and floor tile set clean, sealed, and finished — the detail people actually notice.
  • Plumbing & electrical updates. Sink relocations, island outlets, and new lighting handled in the same job, not subbed out and waited on.

Kitchen remodeling questions

Answered.

Can I use my kitchen while it's being remodeled?

For most of the project, no — once demo starts, the sink, range, and counters are out. We give you the schedule before demo day so you can set up a temporary kitchen, and because one ProCon crew runs the whole job, the down stretch isn't dragged out waiting on outside subs.

Do you move walls and change the layout, or just swap the finishes?

Both. We do layout changes — taking out a wall, adding an island, reworking the work triangle — not just a cabinet-and-counter facelift. Dan has been building in Duluth since 1997, so structural changes are handled in-house rather than handed to a separate contractor.

Who handles the plumbing and electrical when the layout changes?

We do, as part of the same job. Moving a sink, adding outlets for an island, or wiring new lighting are all included in the scope and coordinated by the same crew — so there's no gap where the kitchen sits waiting for a plumber or electrician to show up.

How much does a kitchen remodel cost in Duluth?

There's no flat rate — cabinetry, counter material, tile, and whether walls move all drive the number, so a galley refresh in Proctor and a gutted open-concept in Duluth aren't priced the same. We give you a free written estimate for your kitchen and the selections you choose.

Will one crew handle the whole kitchen, or do I coordinate trades?

One crew, start to finish — you don't line up a demo crew, a cabinet installer, a tile setter, and an electrician yourself. Dan is on site through it, so there's one person accountable for the whole kitchen, in Duluth, Hermantown, Cloquet, or Proctor.
