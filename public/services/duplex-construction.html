The build

Twice the systems,
twice the code.

A duplex isn't a house with a wall down the middle. It's two of nearly everything — two plumbing systems, two electrical services, two HVAC setups — separated by a wall that has to carry a fire rating and hold back sound. That doubling, and the multi-family code that comes with it, is exactly where a duplex goes wrong with a builder who hasn't run one.

Dan Bruckelmyer quotes the project, details the party wall, and is on the site while both units go up. You get one person answering for the whole structure instead of trades pointing at each other across the shared wall. Here's what a ProCon duplex includes:

  • Foundation, sized for the full structure. One footprint poured and leveled to carry both units before anything frames up.
  • Framing for two units. The structure and shell that carry a multi-family build through Northern Minnesota seasons.
  • Party-wall & fire separation. The wall between units detailed to code for fire rating and sound — not treated like an interior partition.
  • Two of each system. Separate plumbing, electrical, and HVAC for each unit, coordinated and overseen as one job.
  • Insulation past code minimums. Wall and attic assemblies built beyond the Minnesota minimum — quieter units and lower bills on both sides.
  • Finish on both units, to the same standard. Trim, cabinetry, and tile completed on each side — no “rental-grade” corner cutting.

Duplex questions

Answered.

How long does it take to build a duplex in Duluth?

A duplex typically takes 10 to 14 months from foundation to final walkthrough — longer than a single-family home because you're building two of every system behind one roof. You get a real schedule for both units before the first pour.

What's different about building a duplex versus a single-family home?

Two of nearly everything: two plumbing systems, two electrical services, two HVAC setups, and a code-rated party wall between them for fire separation and sound. That added code and coordination is the whole reason a duplex needs a builder who has run them — in Duluth, that's ProCon, licensed under #QB807406.

Can you build a multi-generational home where two units share a roof?

Yes. A shared multi-generational build is one of the most common reasons people in Hermantown and Cloquet call us — separate living space for parents or adult kids, with the party wall handling sound and fire separation. Both units get finished to the same standard.

Is the party wall really that important?

It's the part of a duplex most likely to be done wrong. The party wall carries the fire-separation rating between units and most of the sound control, so we detail and build it to code rather than treating it like an interior partition. It's also where insulation past the Minnesota minimum pays off — quieter units and lower heating bills on both sides through a Duluth winter.

Do you build rentals as well as owner-occupied duplexes?

Both. Whether you're building to rent both sides, live in one and rent the other, or house family, the construction is the same and so is the guarantee. We build duplex and multi-family projects across Duluth, Hermantown, Cloquet, and Proctor.
