The space you're missing

Cars out of the
snow, finally.

It usually starts the same way: there's no room for the shop, the second vehicle lives under a foot of January snow, and whatever garage exists looks nothing like the house. A real detached garage fixes all three at once — and it's permanent square footage you own, not a tent or a rented bay across town.

We start at the standard 24-by-24 two-car and size up from there: extra depth for a workbench wall, a heated shop, a third bay, or a loft over the cars for the things you only need half the year. Here's what a ProCon garage build includes:

  • Slab or foundation. Poured and set first — the flat, level base the whole building stands on for decades.
  • Framing. The structure squared and braced to carry a Northern Minnesota snow load, not just hold a roof up.
  • Roofing matched to your home. Shingles and rooflines that read as part of the house, not a separate add-on.
  • Siding matched to your home. Exterior that ties the garage to your existing siding instead of standing out beside it.
  • Overhead & service doors. The garage doors you drive through and the walk-in door you actually use day to day.
  • Optional shop space or loft. Extra depth or a loft worked into the design up front, so the slab and framing are right for it.

Garage & outbuilding questions

Answered.

How big is a standard two-car garage?

A standard two-car garage is typically 24 feet wide by 24 feet deep — two vehicles with a little room around them. In Duluth we build that size most often, but we also build larger garages and outbuildings when you want a third bay, a workshop, or a loft.

How long does it take to build a detached garage?

Most detached garages are built in four to eight weeks once the slab is in. The range depends on size, the door and window layout, and weather — a straightforward 24×24 in Hermantown goes faster than a larger building with a finished loft.

Will the new garage match my house?

Yes — that's the point of building it rather than ordering a kit. We match the roofing and siding to your existing home so the garage reads as part of the property, not a metal box dropped beside it. We work with your current materials wherever they can still be sourced.

Can you add a shop or a loft to the garage?

Yes. Plenty of ProCon garages in Cloquet and Proctor include extra depth for a workbench wall, a heated shop area, or a loft over the bays for seasonal storage. We size those into the design up front so the slab, framing, and stairs are right from the start.

Do you handle the slab and the permit?

Yes. ProCon pours the slab or sets the foundation and pulls the permit — it's one job with one contractor, licensed in Minnesota under #QB807406 and insured. You're not coordinating a concrete crew, a framer, and a door company yourself.
